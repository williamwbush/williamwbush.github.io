Since the pandemic began, I have really gotten into birdwatching.

With birdwatching you can:

  • Explore your city and environment.
  • Help advance science and aid conservation efforts.
  • Make friends with other eccentric birdwatchers.
  • Exercise!
  • Just get away from it all!!

Birdwatching is a great activity for people of all ages. Even if you're sitting at home looking out the window at the birds in the trees or at your feeder, you can still participate in this lifetime activity.

When you go birdwatching for the first time, or birding, as more serious birders like to call it, here are a few tips to help you get started.

  1. Start in an ideal habitat and at the best time of day for the birds you are seeking.
  2. Stop, look and listen.
  3. Be extremely loud as quiet as possible.
  4. Sigh disappointedly when you miss a bird. Be patient.
  5. You won't be able to identify every bird. That's normal. If you can, take pictures to help identify birds later if you don't have time to look them up in your birding app or field guide.
